Andrej Karpathy（前 Tesla AI 总监，OpenAI 创始团队，Stanford PhD）

A fascinating experiment on LLM persuasion: draft a blog post, spend 4 hours using an LLM to sharpen the argument until it feels bulletproof, then ask the LLM to argue the opposite. It promptly demolishes the entire argument and convinces you the opposite is true. The lesson: LLMs are extremely competent at arguing almost any direction, making them genuinely useful for forming your own opinions — but be careful with their inherent sycophancy. The line between "eliciting an opinion" and "manufacturing one" is thinner than it looks.

LLM 在说服力上不分正反：先花 4 小时用 LLM 把文章论点打磨得无懈可击，再让它反驳同一主题，LLM 轻松推翻了整个论证。结论是：LLM 极擅长论证任意方向，是形成观点的好工具，但也要警惕其谄媚本性——"引发观点"和"制造观点"之间只有一线之隔。

Swyx（Cognition / Temporalio / LatentSpace Pod）

Five years of deliberate avoidance of false equivalence — and for good reason: the world doesn't operate fairly, power laws compound. Zuck's version cost him Google DeepMind; in strategy, equal weight on a, b, c, and d kills focus. False equivalence killed his first devtools startup and plagues policy and talent management. The rules that matter: bet small and deliberately, maintain reversibility, set triggers to catch being wrong early, and if you're more right than expected — double down hard.

刻意回避"虚假对等"五年了，因为世界本质不公平，幂律法则在各处都在叠加。Zuck 因此失去了 Google DeepMind；在战略和内容上把 a、b、c、d 均等对待则是慢性自杀。Swyx 的规则：谨慎下注，保持可逆性，设监控触发点，方向对了就狠狠加注。

Amjad Masad（Replit CEO）

Four years ago he started talking about "the 1000x engineer." Now the thesis is playing out: AI lowers the barrier for everyone to build apps, which means the best engineers shift up a layer to build the platforms themselves and expand what's possible. It's a compounding productivity reframe, not just automation.

四年前他提出"千倍工程师"概念，如今正在应验：AI 让每个人都能构建应用，顶级的工程师则往上走一层，去构建平台本身、拓宽可能性的边界。这是关于生产力复利的思考框架，而非简单的自动化叙事…
