## 推1 — Hook

```
4 months ago, I walked away from a company doing tens of millions in annual revenue.

My peers called it career suicide. Especially in this economy.

But I noticed a structural shift in the market that most people are missing.

Here's the contrarian bet I'm making (and why it matters for every solo builder):

(I'm Lynne. Ex-CEO, now building @GenGrowthAI in public. 🧵)
```

---

## 推2 — 拆解认知误区

```
The hidden assumption in "why would you leave?" is:

Revenue scale = value creation ability

That equation only holds when:
• Markets are still growing
• Scale turns into moats
• Your time scales with org size

All three were failing for me.
```

---

## 推3 — 个人诚实的代价

```
Here's what most CEOs won't admit:

As the company scaled to tens of millions in revenue, my marginal contribution was actually shrinking.

Not because I worked less.
Because I spent more time on:
→ Coordinating department politics
→ Managing complexity at scale
→ Maintaining a bloated org

I had become an expensive cog. Not the engineer building new machines.
```

---

## 推4 — 核心洞察：稀缺性转移

```
So what did I actually see?

Everyone talks about AI unlocking indie hackers.

But that's the obvious layer.

The real insight: AI didn't solve the "build a product" problem.

It moved the desperation.

From "I can't build it" → "I built it and no one showed up."

Building is now cheap. Distribution is still brutally hard.
```

---

## 推5 — 市场结构，第一手数据

```
Spent a few months observing and talking to indie developers.

Same story, every time:

"I built the product in 2 hours using AI.
I've spent 200 hours since trying to get anyone to see it.
Still basically zero users."

That gap — between building and being found — is now the real scarcity.

And almost no one is building for it.
```

---

## 推6 — 为什么这个问题还没有被解决

```
"But what about existing growth tools?"

Some are working on it. It's early.

The distribution gap for indie builders is real —
and the solutions are still catching up.

That's the problem we're going after.
```
